tl;dr|

Summarize content easily

tl;dr is a program that takes a document , whether it's a research paper or a news article, and locates the key components to the text. Most of the time, documents have extra content that pays little service when a reader is trying to quickly read through a document and get a general overview of it.

tl;dr parses text and summarizes it through a series of context analysis and text parsing. The program is developed using with the ideas of linguistics and modern essay writing to narrow which clauses within the document which describe the main idea the most. Instead of using artificial learning or machine learning, this program uses hard coded rules. These rules are programmed in to analyze the structure and format , and works for most essay structures. As this algorithm uses hard-coded systems, bias can be reduced and the amount of content displayed can be systematically adjusted.

Once a document has been uploaded, he program will pick out which clauses contain the most relevant information that the document is trying to convey. Clauses are automatically chosen based off their relevance and consistency with the rest of the document. These ideas will be automatically organized by the program to inform the reader as much as possible about the subject.

Key ideas may be isolated into a point form notation or by fading the surrounding words, based on what the user chooses. No matter which option they use, context can be read by simply hovering the mouse over the isolated idea. For point form, it will point to which part on the document contains the context, while the other option may increase opacity of the document around that point.
